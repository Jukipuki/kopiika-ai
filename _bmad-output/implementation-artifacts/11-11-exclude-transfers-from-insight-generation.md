# Story 11.11: Exclude transfers (and other non-spending kinds) from insight generation

Status: ready-for-dev

<!-- Note: Validation is optional. Run validate-create-story for quality check before dev-story. -->

## Story

As a **user uploading a statement dominated by transfers (or any non-spending activity)**,
I want the Teaching Feed to stop generating multiple LLM insight cards that restate "transfers dominate your activity",
so that my feed reflects my actual consumption and — when transfers really do dominate — I see exactly one clear, structural card acknowledging that, not four redundant paragraphs.

## Context

Post-Epic-11 UAT surfaced a concrete failure: a PE account upload where 97% of activity was transfers produced 4 LLM-generated insight cards all restating the same idea. Root cause: [backend/app/agents/education/node.py:20-52](backend/app/agents/education/node.py#L20) (`_build_spending_summary`) filters only by **amount sign** (`amount >= 0`), so transfers (which have negative amounts but `transaction_kind='transfer'`) are treated as spending and dominate the aggregated totals fed to the LLM. Epic 11 Story 11.2 made `transaction_kind` a first-class field on `categorized_transactions` (already in state — see [backend/app/agents/state.py:9](backend/app/agents/state.py#L9)), so the fix is a kind-based filter plus a single deterministic card for the dominant-transfers edge case.

This story is analogous to Story 4.9 (savings ratio wired to `transaction_kind`): a small, surgical consumer change that reads `transaction_kind` directly instead of inferring from amount signs. No schema change, no migration, no API change. Filed under Epic 11 (not Epic 3, where the code physically lives) because it's a direct consumer of Story 11.2's `transaction_kind` field and was surfaced by Epic 11 UAT.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **Given** a set of `categorized_transactions` where some entries have `transaction_kind='spending'` and others have `transaction_kind` in `{income, savings, transfer}`, **When** `_build_spending_summary` runs, **Then** only entries with `transaction_kind == 'spending'` contribute to the top-category totals and the "Total spending" figure; entries with other kinds are excluded from those totals.

2. **Given** the same input, **When** `_build_spending_summary` builds its output string, **Then** the returned summary appends a **"(excluded from analysis)"** footer listing per-kind totals for the excluded kinds in the form `- <kind>: ₴<uah>` (one line per non-spending kind present, in deterministic order: `income`, `savings`, `transfer`; kinds with zero total are omitted). If no non-spending kinds are present the footer is omitted entirely.

3. **Given** a pipeline run where the sum of `abs(amount)` for `transaction_kind != 'spending'` exceeds **70%** of the sum of `abs(amount)` across all categorized transactions (the "mostly-transfers" threshold), **When** `education_node` assembles cards, **Then** exactly **one** deterministic, non-LLM "mostly-transfers" structural card is prepended to the feed (after subscription cards, before LLM cards), with a localized headline, a `key_metric` showing the transfer share as a percentage, `why_it_matters`, `deep_dive`, `severity='info'`, `category='transfers'`, and `card_type='structuralCard'`; the LLM is NOT asked to produce a card on this topic.

4. **Given** the "mostly-transfers" threshold is met, **When** the LLM generates its cards, **Then** the LLM still runs on the spending-only summary (it may legitimately have nothing to say and return zero cards — that's acceptable); no second transfer-themed card is generated by any means.

5. **Given** the "mostly-transfers" threshold is **not** met (i.e. transfers are ≤70% of activity), **When** `education_node` assembles cards, **Then** **no** deterministic mostly-transfers card is emitted — only subscription cards and LLM cards are returned.

6. **Given** the 4 prompt templates in [backend/app/agents/education/prompts.py](backend/app/agents/education/prompts.py) (`ENGLISH_BEGINNER_PROMPT`, `ENGLISH_INTERMEDIATE_PROMPT`, `UKRAINIAN_BEGINNER_PROMPT`, `UKRAINIAN_INTERMEDIATE_PROMPT`), **When** the LLM receives any of them, **Then** each template contains a single explicit instruction forbidding insights about transfer volume, income volume, or savings volume — wording like "Do NOT generate insights about transfer, income, or savings volume. These are shown as context only, and a separate card already handles the mostly-transfers case when relevant."

7. **Given** Epic 11 Story 11.2 is live, **When** `education_node` receives `categorized_transactions` from the categorization node, **Then** each entry carries a `transaction_kind` field (values ∈ `{spending, income, savings, transfer}`); `_build_spending_summary` and the mostly-transfers detector read this field directly and do NOT infer kind from category labels, amount signs, or MCC codes. Entries missing the `transaction_kind` key are treated as `'spending'` (matches the DB default and preserves pre-11.2 behaviour for any legacy caller).

8. **Given** `categorized_transactions` is empty OR contains only non-spending kinds (e.g. a statement containing nothing but transfers), **When** `education_node` runs, **Then** it does not crash; if all activity is non-spending and the 70% threshold is met, the mostly-transfers card is emitted and the LLM is not invoked on an empty spending summary (short-circuit: skip the RAG retrieval + LLM call when the spending-only total is zero, since any output would be noise).

9. **Given** an integration fixture representing the real regression (~97% transfers, a handful of spending transactions), **When** the full pipeline runs through `education_node`, **Then** the output `insight_cards` list contains **exactly one** card with `card_type='structuralCard'` and `category='transfers'`, and **zero** LLM-generated cards whose headline or body mentions transfers (assertion: no card except the structural one may contain the word "transfer"/"переказ" in headline, key_metric, why_it_matters, or deep_dive).

## Dev Notes

### Why kind-filtering, not another amount-sign trick

Epic 11 Story 11.2 made `transaction_kind` the single source of truth for "what is this transaction economically". Amount signs are a proxy that falls apart on real data — transfers are negative on the outgoing leg, positive on the incoming leg, and the existing filter (`if amount >= 0: continue`) kept all outgoing transfers while dropping incoming ones. That's both half-right and statistically catastrophic: on a PE-account upload where every transfer appears as a negative line on the statement side we're analysing, the outgoing legs dominate and the LLM sees "all spending is transfers". The fix aligns with Story 4.9's approach: downstream consumers read `transaction_kind` directly.

### Why a 70% threshold (not 50%, not 80%)

- 50% would fire too often — a user with a large rent/mortgage transfer and normal spending would still trigger it.
- 80% felt too high — the UAT regression was 97% but a 75/25 split is still functionally "mostly transfers" for the purpose of telling the user "your spending insights are computed on the remaining 25%".
- 70% gives comfortable headroom above "roughly half" and fires confidently on the regression case. The constant lives in one place (`MOSTLY_TRANSFERS_THRESHOLD`) so tuning is trivial.

### Why a structural card, not an LLM prompt-steering card

LLM cards on "you have lots of transfers" are (a) redundant with one-another and (b) structurally the same user-facing message every time. A deterministic card is cheaper, always correct, and capped at one. The subscription-cards pattern in `_build_subscription_cards` is the existing precedent for deterministic, pre-rendered cards in this node.

### Ordering in `all_cards`

Final order: `subscription_cards + structural_cards + llm_cards`. Rationale:
1. Subscription alerts are most actionable (hidden recurring costs) → top of feed.
2. Structural cards explain the context of what follows ("here's why there are fewer insights below") → before LLM cards.
3. LLM cards are educational, variable-quality → last.
